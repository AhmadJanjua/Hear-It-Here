Nas - Illmatic

Often cited as one of the best hip-hop albums of the '90s, Illmatic is a hardcore hip hop album with multi-syllabic internal rhymes and inner-city narratives. Notable songs include “It Ain’t Hard to Tell,” “The World is Yours,” “One Love,” “Halftime,” and “Life’s a Bitch.”

Micheal Jackson - Thriller

Thriller is the biggest-selling album of all time with over 70 million copies sold worldwide. Jackson aimed to create an album where “every song was a killer”. The album features a mix of R&B, pop, rock, funk and ballads. It turned MJ into a megastar and led him straight to the throne as the King of Pop

Kendrick Lamar - To Pimp a Butterfly

A staple of modern hip hop, To Pimp a Butterfly is incorporates jazz, funk, and soul. It is Kendrick Lamar’s third studio album. Notable songs include “King Kunta,” “Alright,” “These Walls,” “I,” and “The Blacker the Berry.”

The Weekend - Kiss Land

Kiss Land is The Weeknd’s debut studio album, released on September 10, 2013. The album features the lead single “Kiss Land” and other popular tracks such as “Belong to the World” and “Live For”. The album explores themes of love, sex, and fear through a blend of R&B and dark wave

Duke Elignton & John Coltrane

Duke Ellington & John Coltrane is a jazz album released in 1963 and showcased the rising John Coltrane alongside the established piano institution Duke Ellington. The pairing resulted in a casual, respectful, and generous jazz. Notable songs include "In A Sentimental Mood" and "My Little Brown Book".

Soundtracks

Soundtrack is a term used for a selection of songs that are featured in a film or other visual media. These songs have become a category of their own, with increasingly high-quality music being composed. Notable soundtracks include "God of War Ragnorok", "Inception", "LA Noire" and "The Road to Perdition"
